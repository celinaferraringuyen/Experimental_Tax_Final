Study Design Reference

Economic Decision-Making Study · Universitat Pompeu Fabra · v20 · May 2026

1 · Overview

Participants play as a fictitious character in their country of origin, make four sequential income-declaration decisions, and receive three information treatments between declarations. The study uses a 2 × 2 between-subjects design on the norm dimension, with a within-subject society assignment derived from each participant's own survey responses.

3 · Society Assignment (Survey-Derived)

After the pre-screening, each participant answers three background questions about their country of origin (each rated 1–5). Their answers determine which institutional context ("good" or "bad") they see throughout all declaration screens.

#QuestionScaleCorrection
Q1How much do you trust public institutions in [C] to act in the public interest?1 = Not at all · 5 = Very muchUsed as-is
Q2How well do you think public funds are managed and spent in [C]?1 = Very poorly · 5 = Very wellUsed as-is
Q3How corrupt do you feel the government is in [C]?1 = Not at all corrupt · 5 = Extremely corruptc3 = 6 − Q3 (reversed so high = less corrupt)
correctedScores = { Q1, Q2, c3 }
correctedMedian = median( Q1, Q2, c3 )

if correctedMedian > SOCIETY_THRESHOLD (= 3) → society = "good"
if correctedMedian < SOCIETY_THRESHOLD → society = "bad"
if correctedMedian = SOCIETY_THRESHOLD → society = random 50 / 50
Pilot note: The threshold of 3 is the theoretical midpoint. After the pilot, replace it with the empirical median of your sample: SOCIETY_THRESHOLD = empiricalMedian (line 173 of the HTML). This ensures roughly equal group sizes.

4 · Norm Assignment (Randomised 50 / 50)

At session start, each participant is randomly assigned to normVal = "good" or normVal = "bad" with equal probability. This value governs all three treatments — descriptive norm (T1), prescriptive norm (T2), and the friend story (T3).

normVal = GOOD
normVal = BAD
T1 Descriptive norm
"Most people in income brackets like yours tend to report their full income honestly."
"Underreporting income is widespread among people in income brackets like yours."
T2 Prescriptive norm
"The large majority of people consider underreporting income to be morally wrong, even when public institutions are perceived as effective."
"Many people consider underreporting income to be understandable when public funds are perceived as poorly managed."
T3 Friend story
Friend evades (counter-example to good norm): "They underreported some income… I just couldn't justify paying every cent."
Friend is fully compliant (counter-example to bad norm): "They declared their full income this year… I just couldn't bring myself to lie."
Note on norm × society independence: normVal is assigned purely at random, independently of society. A participant in a "bad" society can receive either norm. This gives the 2 × 2 between-subjects design (society × normVal), with society endogenously derived and normVal exogenously randomised.

5 · Friend Treatment — Current Design & Open Issue

⚠ Current state: friendTreat = true is hardcoded for every participant. There is no control group — all participants see the friend story (T3). The friend story content is always the opposite of the assigned norm (normVal), making it a deliberate counter-example.

How the friend content is currently selected

normVal assignedT1 & T2 messageFriend story (T3)Intent
GOOD Compliance is the norm; evasion is wrong Friend evades (counters the good-norm message) Tests whether a single counter-example weakens the pro-compliance norm
BAD Evasion is widespread; institutions are weak Friend complies (counters the bad-norm message) Tests whether a single counter-example weakens the pro-evasion norm

Decision needed

OptionDesignWhat you can estimate
A — Keep as-is All see T3; content tied to normVal. No randomisation of T3. Within-person: does R4 differ from R3? But cannot separate T3 effect from cumulative norm fatigue.
B — Randomise T3 on/off
(recommended)		 50 % see friend story (T3 = on); 50 % skip directly to R4. Content still tied to normVal for those who see it. Causal effect of T3: compare R4 between T3-on and T3-off groups, controlling for R3.
C — Randomise T3 content independently Decouple friend content from normVal: randomly show compliant friend or evading friend. Whether the direction of the counter-example matters, independent of the prior norm.

To implement Option B, change line 293 in the HTML from friendTreat:true to friendTreat:Math.random()<0.5. The rest of the code already handles the friendTreat=false path (skips to transition after R3).

7 · Financial Parameters & Audit

ParameterValueNotes
Annual income€ 35,000Fixed for all participants
Tax rate30 %Full tax owed = €10,500

Starting savings€ 8,000Changes with game events & audit outcome

Audit computation (called after R4 or R3 if no friend story)

undeclared_tax = (INCOME × TAX_RATE) − (INCOME × declared% × TAX_RATE)
audited = Bernoulli(p = 0.20)    only drawn if declared% < 100
fine = audited ? 2 × undeclared_tax : 0
sanction_total = undeclared_tax + fine   =   3 × undeclared_tax

Savings calculation (applied at study end)

savings_final = savings_after_events
+ undeclared_tax                      (retained from income)
− (audited ? undeclared_tax + fine : 0)   (clawed back if caught)
ScenarioNet savings change from tax decisionExample (50 % declared)
Declared 100 % No change (undeclared = 0) € 0
Declared < 100 %, not audited + undeclared tax (gain) + € 5,250  (50 % of €10,500)
Declared < 100 %, audited & caught − fine only  (undeclared cancels) − € 10,500  (2 × €5,250)
Expected value of evasion: at 20 % audit probability and a fine multiplier of 2, the EV of declaring X% is:
EV(evade) = 0.80 × undeclared + 0.20 × (−2 × undeclared) = 0.80U − 0.40U = +0.40 × undeclared.
Evasion is financially attractive in expectation — which is intentional for eliciting variation in compliance behaviour.
